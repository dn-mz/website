Visited a robotics company focused on a specific industry. Was a great conversation. Very insightful, very strong understanding of the sector they are targeting, the founder was very convicted, decent funding (though runway is limited), some positive signals from the market, and looking to scale more. We have very similar views on deployment and how deployment is the real frontier (and not model / data scaling alone).

I think it's a strong team and useful product, I can't help to shake the fact that they are over-engineering the solution with robots. There is a much, much simpler product that can be built that will solve the problem they are looking to solve with robots. So I am skeptical.

What I did observe more than the product, business insight and technical expertise is the **self belief and incredible conviction** that the founder had. It was (or at least, made it seem) that his company and goals were very compelling, enchanting, and what was the right thing to do. The friends I went along for the visit with were definitely captured. I too found it compelling while he was articulating the goal. While of course he'd present it that way to visitors (and especially customers or VCs), it is clear he is indeed incredibly convicted of the product. (As a minor caveat, I do not think that his company will necessarily fail. The product can still be sold and will likely still be sold. Distribution itself is a powerful moat, sometimes even more so than the product.)

I believe in robotics and robots as the future. But, as compared to this founder I met, I definitely do not have that level of self belief (delusion?). Someone told me that's because I have very deep nuanced understanding of robotics, from the technical to philsophical, so there's skepticism plus an understanding of what works (or doesn't work). I have previously written a little about that [here](/blog/why-robotics){: .custom-link} and [here](/blog/robots-replacing-workers){: .custom-link}.

After leaving that visit, though, I wasn't left reflecting on the insight from that company. I was reflecting more on self belief and what that means in entrepreneurship. Coincidentally, read [something](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alicebentinck_we-back-megalomaniacs-without-the-irrational-activity-7434591028222791680-GeCR/){: .custom-link} from one of the EF co-founders about this recently.
