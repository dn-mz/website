*AI Engineer (15-17 May 2026) was great - very good energy, strong momentum, glad we got things going and going well!*

Just came off a chat with a founder building a great business. Strong product, great customers, funding and revenue. Interestingly, he mentioned something that - that he used to prioritise revenue but over time realised that the better decision is to focus on signals and hype.

Unfortunately, I think his observation is right.

Given the immense uncertainty and risk that VCs work in, revenue is only one signal of many others. VCs cannot deeply diligence every part of a business, so signals become useful shortcuts instead. Momentum and signals become currency in the attention economy.

Fundraising for Servo last year was where I learnt this first land. We had strong operations, real traction and meaningful deployment. But we didn't build up the signals and hype before the fundraise. We did reasonably well by most standards, but I learnt substance alone was not sufficient. It was disorienting for me.

My first company, JustShip, was bootstrapped, profitable and operationally rigorous. We built around customer, product and people from day one; so my instinct was always to focus on the same approach. Focus on fundamentals like revenue, systems, reliability and trust. Built something useful and enduring.

I've seen too much of companies optimising heavily for signals and momentum. It works. Fundraising is achieved, but only to cross a few milestones before realising that there was nothing there in the first place. This isn't the path I want to optimise for.

I'm now trying to find that balance. The best companies have both: strong fundamentals and the ability to communnicate vision clearly. 

If I could pick, I'd rather build durable systems rather than optimise for signals. Might be a slower, more principled approach. Hopefully I'm right that over a sufficiently long timeline, substance > signals.
