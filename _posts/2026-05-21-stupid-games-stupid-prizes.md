*AI Engineer (15-17 May 2026) was great - very good energy, strong momentum, glad we got things going and going well!*

Just came off a chat with a founder. Great product, great business, great clients. Interestingly, one thing he mentioned was that, as a Singaporean founder, he used to prioritise revenue. But what gets people ahead, it's the signals, hype and aura farming. 

The unfortunate part is that, even if I disagree with the principle and philosophy of that, I concede that such is the reality in the VC game.

Fundraising for Servo last year was especially bizarre - we had great performance, traction, insane operations - but we didn't build up the signals and hype before the fundraise. It would not be accurate to say we failed ($8 at $85 is decent), but it was definitely disheartening to learn that hype matters so much more than substance.

Coming from a place where my first company was bootstrapped, post-revenue, and significant profit margins, I want to build a company with strong business fundamentals. I want to build a generational company that makes a difference. It's not to say there isn't an overlap between signals and business fundamentals. But if it's pure aura farming (which I see a lot of)? I don't want to play stupid games, because that'd be seeking stupid prizes.

Maybe I'm too principled, to my detriment.

DNMZ
