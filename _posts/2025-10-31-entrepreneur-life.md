*Sold JustShip two years ago!*

Three key lessons from JustShip:

1) **Co-founder relationships are critically important.** Communicate. Trust. Humility. Goal alignment.

It's really easy to type this, and extremely difficult to get right. A study by HBS professor Noam Wesserman identified that 65% of start-ups fail due to "people problems", specifically conflicts among co-founders. In contrast, 35% of failures are due to market factors or product issues. His research may be slightly dated - I think it was from 2008 - but I won't be surprised if the numbers are similar today.

I think about my experiences at JustShip, and the ups and downs. I'm grateful for my co-founder Roger - we had some very difficult conversations and worked through many things - and am so grateful for the trust we had and now have even more of. It's very, very fun when working with the right co-founder and team.

2) **Data.** Collect as much of it as possible, even things you don't yet analyse. Then, mindfully analyse.

What gets measured gets done. It's be a bit of a double edged sword. But before you even talk about KPIs... collect data. That said, be mindful as too much data can be noise, so it's important to be discerning about what you analyse. Just collect the data, first. It'll come in handy.

And it did for JustShip. Pricing, sales and costs predictions, informed marketing and SEO, how we simplify categories of items, and more. I tracked every expense rigorously, and I'd recommend that for anyone.

3) **Culture and values will bring the company a long way.** Don't compromise.

At JustShip we focused on doing things right. We refunded every cent that was overpayed. We continually reached out to partners to build long, trusting relationships. We took care of packages well. And our customers loved it. We also had a 5 star Google rating because of all this.

That reputation compounds. And it compounds quickly. JustShip's growth was incredible, and I'm grateful for the key values we embedded at the start to ensure the growth was principled.

--

Very grateful for my time at JustShip, and very hopeful for its success even now when I've exited.
